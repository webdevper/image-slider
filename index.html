BLACK PANTHER
In response to the lack of Black characters in comics, Lee and Kirby created T’Challa, the prince of Wakanda, a country with a mix of futuristic technology and traditional life due to its Vibranium resources. After his father's death at the hands of Ulysses Klaw, T’Challa became king and the Black Panther. Enhanced by a mystical herb, T’Challa gained near-superhuman strength and agility. Deciding to use his powers for all humanity, he left Wakanda for New York after meeting the Fantastic Four.

IRONMAN
Iron Man, a popular Marvel hero, is the alter ego of billionaire inventor Tony Stark. Created by Stan Lee, Larry Lieber, Don Heck, and Jack Kirby, Iron Man first appeared in Tales of Suspense #39 in 1963. After being captured and critically injured during a conflict, Stark builds a powered suit of armor to save his life and escape captivity. With advanced technology and his genius intellect, Stark continues to upgrade his suit, using it to fight evil and protect the world as Iron Man. Stark is also a founding member of the Avengers, contributing his resources and intellect to the superhero team.

THOR
Thor, a Marvel hero and Avenger, embodies the Norse God of Thunder. Created in 1962 by Stan Lee, Larry Lieber, and Jack Kirby, he wields Mjolnir, granting flight and lightning control. Thor, son of Odin, defends Earth and Asgard with immense strength, valor, and a noble spirit, battling cosmic threats to protect both realms and humanity.

SPIDERMAN
Spider-Man, a beloved Marvel hero, is the alter ego of high school student Peter Parker. Created by Stan Lee and Steve Ditko, he first appeared in Amazing Fantasy #15 in 1962. Bitten by a radioactive spider, Parker gains agility, strength, and the ability to cling to walls. Balancing his life as a superhero with personal struggles, Spider-Man fights crime in New York City with his web-slinging abilities and quick wit.
